🍲 The Pot — pick a body style

Same character system in every option: dot-eyes + blush + smile, a citrus-yellow wooden spoon, a sprout on the lid (echoing your logo's leaf), and steam = mood. Only the silhouette changes. Each is shown big, plus tiny (48 px) to check it still reads as a glyph.

Option A

Dutch oven

Squat, wide, ear-handles — the Le Creuset / cocotte silhouette. Reads "home cooking & comfort" most strongly. Wide face = very characterful. Cosy-leaning.

48 px
Option B

Stockpot

Tall, upright, loop handles — "big batch / soup pot." More modern and a touch energetic. Taller body gives room for the lid to become a hat or hold props.

48 px
Option C

Cauldron / belly pot

Round pot-belly with tiny legs — the most storybook & cute. Maximum "huggable." Leans whimsical/fun; the rounded belly is the friendliest shape of the four.

48 px
Option D

Saucepan

Single long handle — the most iconic/minimal "cooking" glyph; the handle reads instantly even tiny. The handle is a built-in spot for a hand or a label. Slightly more utilitarian than cute.
