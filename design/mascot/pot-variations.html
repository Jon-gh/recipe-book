🍲 Pot — round 2: Dutch oven vs Cauldron

Fixed the Dutch oven (wide shallow overhanging lid + low squat body + ear handles) and cleaned up the topper. Pick a body from the two families below, then pick a topper from the bottom row — they combine freely.

Dutch oven — 3 takes

A1

Classic

Wide shallow overhanging lid, low body, small ear tabs. The textbook Le Creuset read.

A2

Round belly

Same lid, but the body bulges out — chubbier and friendlier, halfway to the cauldron.

A3

Staub + base

Extra-flat lid, round ball knob, and a little foot/base — the most "real cookware" of the three.

Cauldron / belly pot — 3 takes

C1

Little legs

Round belly on three stubby legs — the storybook cauldron. Most overtly cute & characterful.

C2

Pedestal base

Belly on a small footed base instead of legs — a touch more grown-up, still rounded and warm.

C3

Wide oval

Squatter, wider mouth — closest to the Dutch oven but with the cauldron's soft rounded bottom.

Topper options (combine with any body)

T1

Single leaf

Simplest & cleanest. One tidy leaf — reads well even at icon size.

T2

Two-leaf sprout

The redrawn pair — symmetric, no overlap. "Fresh / growing." The default I'd suggest.

T3

Cherry tomato

A tomato on the vine — adds the tomato-red brand pop right up top. The most playful/foody.

T4

Plain knob

No sprig at all — just a clean knob. Most minimal; loses the leaf tie-in to your logo.
